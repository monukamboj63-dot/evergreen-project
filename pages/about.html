The Story of Jim Corbett

Once upon a time in the wild forests of Kumaon...

There lived a man named Edward James Corbett, born in 1875 in Nainital, at the edge of the Himalayan wilderness. As a young boy, Jim roamed the forests, learning the ways of the jungle and the language of its creatures. He became a legendary hunter, famed for tracking and eliminating man-eating tigers and leopards that threatened villages. But Jim was not just a hunter—he was a keen observer, a storyteller, and a friend to the people and animals of Kumaon.

Jim’s adventures were filled with danger and compassion. He would spend days following pugmarks, listening to the calls of birds, and reading the silent stories written in the dust. His most famous tales, like the Man-Eating Leopard of Rudraprayag and the Champawat Tiger, are still told around campfires today.

But as the years passed, Jim’s heart turned from the rifle to the pen. He became a passionate conservationist, fighting to protect the very animals he once hunted. His books, such as Man-Eaters of Kumaon, brought the magic and mystery of the Indian jungle to the world. In 1957, the park was named in his honor, ensuring that the story of Jim Corbett—the hunter who became a guardian—would never be forgotten.

Jim Corbett National Park

India's first and most famous national park, established in 1936, is a sanctuary for Bengal tigers and a paradise for nature lovers. Located in Uttarakhand, it features lush forests, grasslands, rivers, and a rich diversity of wildlife.

  • Location: Nainital District, Uttarakhand, India
  • Area: 520 sq km
  • Famous for: Bengal Tiger, Asian Elephant, Leopards, Crocodiles, 600+ bird species
  • Best time to visit: November to June

Named after the legendary hunter-turned-conservationist Jim Corbett, the park is a haven for adventure, wildlife, and eco-tourism.

Park Timeline

1936
Hailey National Park Established

Asia's first national park, created to protect the Bengal tiger and other wildlife.

1957
Renamed Corbett National Park

Renamed in honor of Jim Corbett, legendary hunter, author, and conservationist.

1973
Project Tiger Launched

Corbett became the first park under Project Tiger, India's flagship tiger conservation program.

1980s
Ecotourism & Research

Corbett becomes a model for ecotourism and wildlife research in India, attracting scientists and nature lovers worldwide.

Today
Biodiversity Hotspot

Home to 600+ bird species, 50+ mammals, 25+ reptiles, and a global eco-tourism destination.

Wildlife & Attractions

Bengal Tiger

The park is world-famous for its Bengal tigers. Early morning safaris offer the best chance to spot these majestic cats.

Asian Elephant

Large herds of wild elephants roam the park, especially near rivers and grasslands. Elephant safaris are a unique experience.

Leopard

Leopards are elusive but can be seen in the hilly and forested areas of the park.

Deer Species

Chital, sambar, barking deer, and hog deer are commonly seen grazing in the meadows.

Birds

Over 600 species of birds, including peacocks, kingfishers, eagles, and migratory birds, make Corbett a birdwatcher's paradise.

Other Wildlife

The park is also home to crocodiles, otters, sloth bears, wild boar, and many reptiles and amphibians.

Safari Experience

Choose from jeep, canter, or elephant safaris to explore the park's different zones and landscapes.

Scenic Beauty

The park's diverse landscapes include dense forests, open grasslands, rivers, and hills, offering stunning views and photo opportunities.
